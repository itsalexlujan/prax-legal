Prax — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 21, 2026

Prax is built on a simple idea: your practice is yours. The short version: you can use Prax with no account at all — and if you do, your training data never leaves your phone. The only time Prax sends anything to a server is when you choose to use a connected feature: the Circle (the optional social layer, which needs an account), importing a routine, or connecting Spotify. Each is explained below.

1. Using Prax without an account

Prax works fully without signing in. In this mode, everything you record — your practices, movements, sets, weights, reflections, routines, and settings — is stored only on your device. It is never sent to us or anyone else. There is no account, no server call, and nothing for us to see.

2. Photos and camera

If you add a photo to your profile or to a share card, Prax uses your camera or photo library only to create that image on your device. Profile photos and share images stay on your device and are not uploaded to us or any third party. Access is requested only when you use the feature, and you can decline it in your device settings.

3. The Circle (optional — requires an account)

If you choose to use the Circle, Prax's social layer, you create an account and a limited amount of data is stored on our backend (hosted by Supabase) so your connections can see it. This includes:

  • Account identity: when you sign in with Apple, we receive your name and email from Apple. (You can use Apple's "Hide My Email" to share a relay address instead.) If you sign in by email, we receive that email address.
  • Profile: the display name, bio, and any Instagram/X handles you enter, plus your initials.
  • Your "witnessing surface": the limited activity your connections are meant to see — your active days, current and longest continuance (streak), whether you're training right now, your most recent practice's title, and (if you attach one) your most recent song.
  • Connections and invites: who you are connected to, and the invite codes you create.

Only you and people you are mutually connected to can read this data. This is enforced at the database level (row-level security), not just in the app. Your private Archive — the actual movements, sets, weights, and reflections — stays on your device and is never synced, even when you use the Circle.

4. Importing a routine

When you use "bring in a routine," the content you provide (text, an image or photo, a PDF, or another file you upload) is sent to our import service (a Cloudflare Worker), which passes it to OpenRouter and Anthropic (Claude) to read it into a structured routine. This is used only to parse that one routine and return it to you. The import service does not store your submission after processing it. If you'd rather not send content off-device, the app also has an on-device fallback that parses plain text locally.

5. Spotify (optional)

If you connect Spotify, Prax reads what you're currently playing (song title, artist, and album art) so you can attach it to a shared practice. Your Spotify login tokens are stored encrypted on your device and are never sent to us. If you attach a song, that song's title/artist/art becomes part of your witnessing surface and is visible to your connections. You can disconnect Spotify at any time.

6. Analytics, tracking, and advertising

Prax contains no advertising and no third-party ad tracking. We do not build an advertising profile of you and do not sell your data. The app may check our update service (Expo) for app updates, which involves a standard network request.

7. Third-party services we rely on

To provide the connected features above, Prax uses these services as processors:

  • Apple — Sign in with Apple (account identity)
  • Supabase — hosts the Circle database and authentication
  • Cloudflare — runs the routine-import service
  • OpenRouter / Anthropic — read an imported routine into structured data
  • Spotify — provides your currently-playing track (only if you connect it)
  • Expo — delivers app updates

Each processes only the data described in its section above.

8. Children

Prax is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from them.

9. Deleting your data

  • On-device data (your Archive, reflections, photos, settings): delete the app and it is gone. There is no copy anywhere else.
  • Circle account data: you can delete your account and everything associated with it (your profile, witnessing surface, connections, invites, and shared routines) at any time from inside the app: Settings → Delete account. You can also email us at the address below. Deleting your account permanently removes your data from our backend and cannot be undone.

10. Where your data is stored and for how long

Circle data is stored on Supabase's infrastructure and kept while your account is active. When you delete your account, the associated rows are removed. On-device data lives only on your device for as long as the app is installed.

11. Changes to this policy

If a future version of Prax handles data differently, we'll update this policy and note the change here before that change takes effect.

12. Your choices

You control what leaves your device: use Prax without an account to keep everything local, skip the Circle, use the on-device import fallback, and connect or disconnect Spotify whenever you like.
